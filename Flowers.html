Flowers play a crucial role in honey production. They produce nectar, which bees collect using their proboscis. The nectar is stored in the beeâ€™s specialized stomach, known as the honey crop, where enzymes begin breaking it down. There are multiple classifications of honey depending on the floral or non-floral source. Monofloral honey, as the name suggests, is honey produced by single floral sources of nectar. Polyfloral honey, also known as 'Wildflower', is sources from multiple species of floral nectars. The most uncommon type of honey is called Honeydew honey. Honeydew honey consists of any honey produced from trees or the secretions of aphids.

Depending on which species of flower a bee collects nectar from, the honey can have completely different flavor profiles and active compounds.

Flower Type Honey Profile
Clover Sweet, mild hint of spice, good for cooking, baking, tea
Wildflower Flavor will depend on region, generally darker in color and more robust flavors
Alfalfa Less sweet, delicate herbal flavor, great for cooking
Buckwheat Bold earthy flavor, dark in color, good molasses substitute
Rhododendron This special type of honey is native to Nepal and Turkey. Moderately toxic and psychoactive due to the presence of grayanotoxins in the honey. Used as traditional medicine for inflammatory diseases and diabetes
