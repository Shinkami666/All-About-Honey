Hives are of utmost importance to all bees, but none more than honey bees. Their way of life is determined by the size and capacity of the hive in which they reside. Beehives are made of thousands of tightly placed hexagonal cells. When there are many cells together, this is called a 'honeycomb'. These honeycombs are used for the storage of food and housing bee larvae.
